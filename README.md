Retail Commercial Strategy & Loyalty Diagnostics

Executive Summary

Action:
Engineered an end-to-end data pipeline by enriching raw POS data with calculated fields in Excel (Loyalty_Flag, Basket_Size_Category, etc.) to enable efficient SQL aggregation. Built an RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) segmentation model in MySQL using NTILE(4) quartile scoring across 1,000 loyalty customers—classifying them into Elite, Active & Engaged, At-Risk, and Lost/Inactive tiers—and visualized the findings across a multi-page Power BI dashboard.

Outcome:
Revealed that Elite customers (26.3% of the base) drive 44.4% of loyalty revenue, spending 3.5× more than inactive customers. Uncovered significant revenue leakage by identifying 10,694 high-value non-loyalty transactions (baskets >₹500) that mirrored loyalty behaviors, alongside 388 'At-Risk' and 'Lost' customers holding ₹11.86L in historical spend. Additionally, a brand performance analysis across 100 brands flagged 28 underperforming assets contributing just 6.84% to total revenue.

Impact:
Quantified a ₹1.55 Crore revenue attribution opportunity by proposing a targeted enrollment strategy for the top 10% of high-value non-loyalty walk-ins. Projected a ₹2.76L incremental revenue recovery through personalized, time-limited re-engagement campaigns for disengaged cohorts, and modeled a further ₹14.93L uplift by designing targeted "brand discovery" promotions aimed at loyalty customers with no prior purchase history of the underperforming brands.
